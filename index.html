    Hi, my name is Selena Sifuentes Shaffer. I am a recent graduate from Sacred Heart University with a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering with minors in Mathematics, Physics, and Honors. My passion lies in building, problem-solving, and working hands-on with projects, whether that’s developing electronics, refining designs, or experimenting in the makerspace. Alongside academics, I bring leadership and teamwork experience as a three-year captain of Sacred Heart’s Division I Women’s Wrestling Team, where I’ve learned the value of discipline, resilience, and collaboration. I’m also an active member and leader in campus organizations, holding roles such as Co-President of Lambda Delta Xi and Social Media Chair of SHU Innovate. Professionally, I’ve built a diverse skill set through roles in residential life, athletic communications, tutoring, and makerspace support. My technical background includes experience with CAD tools, programming (C, Python), circuit design, 3D printing, and electronics testing. I enjoy combining these technical skills with creativity to bring projects to life—for example, designing and building a fully functional pinball machine for my senior capstone. Originally from San Antonio, Texas, I’ve embraced opportunities both in and out of the classroom at Sacred Heart in Connecticut. I’m eager to continue applying my engineering knowledge, leadership skills, and hands-on approach to innovative projects and future career opportunities.
